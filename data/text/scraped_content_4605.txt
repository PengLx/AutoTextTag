Title: The 50-20-30 rule Content: It is a budget rule to help people reach their financial goals. It states that:You should spend up to 50% of your after-tax income on needs and obligations that you must-have or must-do.The remaining half should be split up between 20% savings and debt repayment and 30% to everything else that you might want.
Title: Needs, wants and savings Content: Needs: these are those bills that you absolutely must pay and are the things necessary for survival (rent or mortgage payments, car payments, groceries, insurance, health care, minimum debt payment, and utilities).Wants: these include all the things you spend money on that are not absolutely essential (dinner and movies out, vacations, electronic gadgets, etc.)Savings: this includes adding money to an emergency fund in a bank savings account, making IRA contributions to a mutual fund account, and investing in the stock market.
